FAQs

What does an open port mean?

An open port indicates that an active service on the server or device is accepting external connections. This means communication is available and applications, websites, or systems can connect through that specific port.

What is a filtered port?

A filtered port usually indicates that a firewall, router, or security system is blocking or restricting external access. In these cases, it is not possible to confirm whether the service is active because requests are prevented from reaching the destination.

Why should I check ports?

Checking ports is essential for diagnosing connectivity issues, confirming that firewall rules are correctly configured, and ensuring that critical services are accessible. This practice helps strengthen network security, identify misconfigurations, and maintain the proper operation of applications and servers.
